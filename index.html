Endangered Species in Australia

Australia contains some of the most unique ecosystems and wildlife in the world, yet many native species are increasingly threatened by environmental change and human activity. Habitat destruction, invasive species, climate change, and ecosystem degradation have all contributed to declining biodiversity. Causes of Endangerment

Australian species face a range of environmental pressures that threaten ecosystem stability and biodiversity. Invasive species and diseases, habitat fragmentation, climate change, altered fire regimes, and ecosystem disruption are among the leading causes of species decline. Different species groups are affected in different ways, with some threats impacting hundreds of species simultaneously.

What is Being Done?

Australia has invested in a range of conservation initiatives aimed at protecting biodiversity and restoring ecosystems. Conservation funding supports programs focused on habitat restoration, climate adaptation, environmental research, reef protection, Indigenous land management, and long-term ecosystem sustainability.
